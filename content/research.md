<div class="ws-page">
  <header class="ws-page-intro"><h1>Research</h1><p class="lead">My research uses chemical tracers and sustained observations to understand where seawater and its constituents come from, how they move, and how ocean boundaries reshape them.</p></header>

  <section class="ws-page-block">
    <h2>Three connected questions</h2>
    <div class="ws-card-grid">
      <article class="ws-card"><span class="number">01</span><h3>Water sources</h3><p>Where are Pacific-derived and river-influenced waters observed in the Arctic basins, and how much is present?</p></article>
      <article class="ws-card"><span class="number">02</span><h3>Boundary exchange</h3><p>How do broad continental shelves, sediments, sea ice, and meltwater influence the chemistry of central basins?</p></article>
      <article class="ws-card"><span class="number">03</span><h3>System change</h3><p>How do circulation and biogeochemical transformations respond as polar ocean and ice conditions change?</p></article>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="ws-page-block ws-feature">
    <img src="/assets/sea-ice.jpg" alt="Arctic sea ice and sky photographed from a research vessel." />
    <div class="ws-feature-copy"><p class="ws-eyebrow">Chemical tracers</p><h2>Signals carried by seawater</h2><p>Gallium, barium, vanadium, methane, nutrients, and related measurements provide complementary views of water mass origin, circulation, biological cycling, and exchange with ocean margins. Taken together, they reveal processes that no single tracer can resolve alone.</p></div>
  </section>

  <section class="ws-page-block">
    <h2>Programs and collaborations</h2>
    <div class="ws-project-grid">
      <article class="ws-project"><h3>Antarctic gallium and sea ice</h3><p>Laura leads an NSF-funded U.S. GEOTRACES project examining dissolved gallium, barium, and vanadium in Antarctica's Amundsen Sea. Samples collected during the 2023–2024 GP17-ANT expedition are being used to investigate water-mass circulation, sediment and glacial inputs, and the ways sea-ice formation and melting affect trace-element cycling. Fieldwork included seawater, snow, and sea-ice sampling across the Amundsen Sea shelf.</p><a href="https://www.nsf.gov/awardsearch/showAward?AWD_ID=2242222" rel="noopener noreferrer">View NSF award 2242222 →</a></article>
      <article class="ws-project"><h3>Icy Seas partnership</h3><p>Whitmore Science partners with Icy Seas Co-Laboratory LLC on scientific computing, reproducible data workflows, modeling, and the development of collaborative ocean-research projects. The partnership brings together expertise in trace-element and sea-ice biogeochemistry with software engineering, data stewardship, and quantitative analysis.</p><a href="https://icyseascolab.io/" rel="noopener noreferrer">Visit Icy Seas →</a></article>
      <article class="ws-project"><h3>New Element XR HR-ICP-MS</h3><p>In 2026, Laura and colleagues at the University of Alaska Fairbanks received an NSF Major Research Instrumentation award to acquire a Thermo Scientific Element XR high-resolution inductively coupled plasma mass spectrometer. The instrument will support precise measurements of trace elements and isotope ratios in environmental samples, enabling research in ocean biogeochemistry, aquaculture, paleoceanography, marine carbon cycling, environmental contaminants, critical minerals, and polar systems.</p><a href="https://www.nsf.gov/awardsearch/showAward?AWD_ID=2511702" rel="noopener noreferrer">View NSF award 2511702 →</a></article>
      <article class="ws-project"><h3>NABOS</h3><p>The Nansen and Amundsen Basins Observational System examines ocean and climate processes across the eastern Arctic. Hydrochemical observations help reveal changes in nutrient dynamics, freshwater distribution, and shelf–basin connectivity.</p><a href="https://uaf-iarc.org/nabos/" rel="noopener noreferrer">Visit NABOS →</a></article>
      <article class="ws-project"><h3>MOSAiC</h3><p>The Multidisciplinary drifting Observatory for the Study of Arctic Climate documented a full annual cycle of the coupled atmosphere, ocean, sea ice, ecosystem, and biogeochemistry. Related landfast-ice work focused on nutrient and trace-metal dynamics during spring melt.</p><a href="https://mosaic-expedition.org/" rel="noopener noreferrer">Visit MOSAiC →</a></article>
      <article class="ws-project"><h3>GEOTRACES</h3><p>International GEOTRACES observations constrain the sources, sinks, and internal cycling of trace elements and isotopes. Work spans Arctic synthesis and Southern Ocean measurements in the Amundsen Sea region.</p><a href="https://www.geotraces.org/" rel="noopener noreferrer">Visit GEOTRACES →</a></article>
      <article class="ws-project"><h3>SEAS the Change</h3><p>This North Pacific Research Board synthesis project combined data compilation, physical models, and linear inverse models to explore the supply and movement of nutrients through Bering and Chukchi Sea ecosystems.</p></article>
