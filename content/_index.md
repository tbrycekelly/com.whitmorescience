      <p class="ws-eyebrow">Research focus</p>
      <p>Ocean chemistry carries the imprint of freshwater, sea ice, sediments, rivers, biological activity, and circulation. Whitmore Science works across those connections—from samples collected at sea to synthesis across ocean basins.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="ws-card-grid">
      <article class="ws-card"><span class="number">01</span><h3>Arctic circulation</h3><p>Using barium, gallium, vanadium, nutrients, and related tracers to distinguish water sources and reveal shelf–basin exchange.</p></article>
      <article class="ws-card"><span class="number">02</span><h3>Sea-ice biogeochemistry</h3><p>Investigating how sea ice stores, transforms, and releases nutrients and trace elements during growth and melt.</p></article>
      <article class="ws-card"><span class="number">03</span><h3>Polar change</h3><p>Combining observations and synthesis to clarify how changing boundaries influence marine chemistry and ecosystems.</p></article>
